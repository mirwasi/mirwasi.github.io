A very cynical guide on how to think like a Bangladeshi

Date: Dec 1, 2019
Reading Time: 2 mins
Section: Notes
Tags: bangladesh values cynical
  1. Do not trust anyone or anything.
  2. Do not care about anything because of #1.
  3. Do not take any initiative unless your life is dependent on it, because of #2.
  4. Be comfortable with the status quo (no matter how painful is that), because of #3.
  5. Grow some kind of tolerance for treachery because of #4.

I had a discussion today with my friend Nafiul, he came up with this couple of gems:

  • Believe that the pursuit of money and status is more important than the pursuit of happiness.
  • Believe that making someone lose in their lives is more important than winning in yours.

Update [Dec 3, 2019]:

After posting this on social media, I got a few interesting feedbacks from my friends:

Nafis T. added,

“Believe that ‘You and your loved ones are alone in this race.'”

which portrays the disconnect between an individual and the society in our country. Individualism, the dictionary definition of which is “the habit or principle of being independent and self-reliant”, is a common feature of western society. But there is this collective belief in the set of values that helps . People do care about policies that the lawmakers make, they have trust and hope on their system.

Avi S. made a brillint change to the second last point. He said:

“…pursuit of money and status is not just more important than pursuit of happiness, but happiness = money + status.”

Instead of the “more than” part, he said the relationship is should be viewed as a equaltity relationship. Additionally, he pointed out that, these points do not relate to each other in the rigid mathematical sense.

Two of my non-Bangladeshi friends commented that these things are quite common in the their countries.
